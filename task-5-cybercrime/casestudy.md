# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common cybercrime where scammers trick users into sending money or revealing sensitive banking details. With the rapid growth of digital payments, such frauds have increased significantly in recent years.

A typical UPI fraud happens in several steps. First, the scammer contacts the victim through a phone call, SMS, or social media, pretending to be a bank official, customer support agent, or even a buyer/seller. Next, they create urgency or offer an attractive deal to gain trust. Then, they send a fake payment request or ask the victim to scan a QR code. The victim, believing they will receive money, unknowingly authorizes a payment instead. In some cases, scammers also ask for OTPs or UPI PINs, which leads to unauthorized transactions.

Students, elderly people, and individuals who are new to digital payments are the most common targets because they may lack awareness about such scams. The consequences can be serious, including financial loss, stress, and loss of trust in online systems. In many cases, recovering the lost money is difficult.

To prevent such fraud, users should never share their UPI PIN or OTP, avoid scanning unknown QR codes, and verify the authenticity of any request before making payments.
